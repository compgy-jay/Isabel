About Me

in which the poet introduces herself

I'm Isabella — a keeper of quiet mornings, a collector of half-light, and a poet who believes that every silence holds a syllable waiting to be born.

I write because the world is too vast for prose. My verses wander through loss, longing, love, and the strange geography of the human heart. Each poem is a small room I build with words — and you are invited in.

When I'm not stitching metaphors, you'll find me reading old letters, watching rain streak the window, or searching for the perfect last line.
